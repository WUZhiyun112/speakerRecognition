Project Aim & Methodology

This project aims to research and develop automatic recognition and processing technologies suitable for multi-speaker conversations in "open scenarios"[cite: 15]. The goal is to precisely answer the question of "who said what and when"[cite: 17].

Our end-to-end framework comprises four core modules[cite: 46]:

  • Speech Preprocessing: Using VAD to extract valid speech segments and remove silence[cite: 48].
  • Speaker Diarization & Verification: Utilizing D-TDNN backbone with Context-Aware Masking (CAM++) to concentrate on target speakers and suppress noise[cite: 50].
  • Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR): Fusing diarization outputs with ASR to generate structured "Speaker ID + Timestamp + Text" transcripts[cite: 55, 56].
  • Dialogue Structuring: Organizing the transcribed text chronologically and grouped by individuals[cite: 57].
