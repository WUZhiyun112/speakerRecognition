# Research on Multi-speaker Dialogue Content Recognition Technology in Open Scenarios ![Status](https://img.shields.io/badge/Status-Active-brightgreen) ![Tech](https://img.shields.io/badge/Tech-PyTorch%20%7C%20Deep%20Learning-blue) ## Project Overview In open scenarios (e.g., corporate meetings, sales communications), traditional single-speaker speech recognition systems struggle to accurately distinguish and transcribe dialogue content due to alternating speakers, overlapping speech, and complex background noise. This project aims to develop an end-to-end multi-speaker dialogue content recognition system to precisely solve the core problem of **"who said what and when."** By deeply integrating Voice Activity Detection (VAD), speaker verification based on context-aware masking, and Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR), the system strives to achieve high-precision multi-speaker diarization and transcription under constrained computational resources. ## Core Technologies This project conducts deep architectural optimization on traditional acoustic models to enhance both accuracy and efficiency: * **Context-Aware Masking (CAM/CAM++)**: Empowers the model to dynamically "focus on the target speaker and blur background noise," extracting highly robust speaker embeddings. * **Squeeze-and-Excitation (SE) Block**: Introduces a channel attention mechanism in the frontend. By capturing global context information for adaptive feature recalibration, it enhances the representation of critical acoustic features. * **Depthwise Separable Convolution**: Reconstructs the backend backbone network for lightweight optimization.
